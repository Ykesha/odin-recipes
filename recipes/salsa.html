Salsa

Description

Salsa is used as a condiment for tacos and other Mexican and Mexican-American foods. It may also be used as a dip for tortilla chips. Salsa may be either raw or cooked, and is typically served at room temperature.

Although the word salsa means "sauce" in Spanish, in English, it refers specifically to different kids of Mexican table sauces, such chunky tomato-and-chili-based pico de gallo, and salsa verde.

Ingredients

    1 lb of Roma tomatoes
    1 14oz can of crushed tomatoes
    1/2 cup of red onion
    1 full jalapeno pepper
    1/4 cup of red bell pepper
    1/4 cup of green bell pepper
    1/4 cup of apple
    2 green onion
    1/3 cup of cilantro
    2 Tbsp of lime juice
    1/2 tsp of garlic powder
    1/2 tsp of onion powder
    1 Tbsp of chillie powder
    1 tsp of cumin
    1 Tbsp of honey
    1/2 cup of olive oil

Steps

  1. Combine ingredients in a food processor and pulse in 1 second burts until ingredients are finely chopped
  2. Place salsa in fridge for two to three hours to allow flavors to marinate
  3. Remove salsa from fridge and serve
  4. Salsa will stay good for up to one week
